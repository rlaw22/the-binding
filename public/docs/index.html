The Player Experience

  1. Voice greeting — "You're about to enter a world of monsters, magic, and choices that matter."
  2. Narrative character creation — no forms, just dialogue.
  3. Pick an adventure — difficulty auto-calibrates to your level.
  4. Each turn: AI narrates → 4 actions + open text → dice → consequences → scene image → coin notification.
  5. Chapter ends with coin breakdown: "You earned 340/500. Creative Problem Solving (+120)…"
  6. Adventure ends — Bronze / Silver / Gold / Platinum tier awarded.
  7. Coins → $BINDING at tier-weighted rates → Paxeer wallet.

Monetization Philosophy

"Generous free, irresistible premium."

No paywalls. 5-hour trial, then $1.99. The coin and $BINDING economy creates play-to-earn that doesn't feel like crypto. The only way to get $BINDING is to play.

🎲 Game Design Decisions

Confirmed design decisions for The Binding's core gameplay systems.

1. Coin & XP System

Every adventure has a theoretical maximum coin pool — fixed, based on story length and difficulty. Coins are earned by how intelligently you play.

ElementDecision
Scoring methodHybrid — real-time subtle notifications + full breakdown per chapter
What earns coinsIntelligence-weighted: creativity, problem-solving, investigation, roleplay, combat efficiency
DistributionBell curve — only 0.01% of players ever max out a single adventure
Post-chapter summary"You earned 340/500. Creative Problem Solving (+120), Investigation (+90)…"
The scoring rubric must be generous — better to reward too often than too rarely at launch.

2. Tier System

TierRequirementRarity
BronzeComplete the adventure~95% of players
SilverEarn 60%+ of max coins~60%
GoldEarn 85%+ of max coins~15%
Platinum95%+ coins AND top 10% completion time~1%
Platinum = Mastery. Speed requirement only applies to Platinum. It's the screenshot people share — the badge that makes someone ask "how did you do that?"

3. Universal Cross-Book Currency

Coins are fungible — 1 Dracula coin = 1 Frankenstein coin. Your lifetime balance carries across all adventures, The Shoppe, and $BINDING conversion.

5. Replayability Engine — Three Layers

LayerWhat ChangesQuality
1: Curated Backbone20–30 hand-crafted scenes per adventureGuaranteed — the skeleton never breaks
2: Procedural TissueAI-generated transitions, side encounters, sub-plotsCurated templates → free gen over time
3: Surface VariationNPC personality, dialogue, item descriptionsEasy for LLMs — always different

The AI DM builds a Player Profile tracking combat affinity, diplomacy, investigation, creativity, risk tolerance, and compassion — then uses it to stretch weak spots, match style, and escalate challenge. Adaptation is invisible.

6. The Shoppe

Three properties per item: Cosmetic (profile display), Gameplay Bonus (small cross-adventure utility), Tradeable NFT (on-chain, sellable).

Anti-pay-to-win: No item replaces playing well. The Shoppe rewards collection and curation, not spending power. Seasonal scarcity creates urgency.

7. Dynamic Difficulty — Rubber-Band Scaling

Fight TypeFrequencyPurpose
Standard70%Scales to your level. Constant engagement
Power Window20%Below your level. You feel like a badass
Challenge Spike10%Above your level. Think creatively or die

8. Pre-Adventure Difficulty

Character level sets baseline difficulty before you start. Same story, same beats — but enemies scaled, puzzles harder, DCs higher. The difficulty is invisible — narratively explained by the AI DM, never shown as a number.

Difficulty is story, not a number. "This Dracula is really hard this time" beats "the game scaled enemies to my level."
