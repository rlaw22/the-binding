# The Binding — Logo / Key Art Commission Brief

## What We Need
A single high-quality illustration to serve as the logo/key art for "The Binding" — an AI-powered RPG platform where players play through classic books.

## The Scene

### Setting
An old wooden desk. Dark, warm, candlelit atmosphere — like a scholar's study at night. Everything has an aged, literary, slightly worn feel. Think antique wood grain, warm shadows, rich amber tones.

### Center of the Desk
A thick, ornate leather-bound book lying **flat on its back** (cover facing up, spine away from viewer). The book's **binding is prominently visible** — the spine, the stitching, the leather texture of the binding running along the edge should be a focal point. This is a heavy, old, well-made book — the kind that pulls you in.

On the book's cover, in elegant but **clearly legible** script/calligraphy: **"The Binding"**

### Right Side of the Desk
A tall, lit candle. This is the primary light source. Warm golden light spilling across the book and the desk surface, casting soft shadows. The candle should feel old — maybe a brass or iron candleholder, partially melted wax.

### Left Side of the Book
A **paper human figure** in a Japanese origami/kirigami paper-fold style. This figure represents **the player** — a person made of folded paper, standing beside the desk, facing the book's binding. They're looking at it, about to open it. The character should feel like a paper craft creation with visible folds, creases, and geometric cuts — NOT a flat silhouette, but a three-dimensional paper-fold figure. Think Japanese kirigami meets paper doll.

### Above the Book (Floating Upward)
Four **paper-craft objects** rising from the pages of the open book, like the imagination inside the book is coming to life. All four should be in the **same origami/paper-fold style** as the human figure. They should be staggered at different heights, drifting upward:

1. **A paper castle** — medieval towers, battlements, all made of folded paper
2. **A paper pirate ship** — sails, hull, rigging, all in paper folds
3. **A paper dragon** — wings outstretched, tail curled, made of geometric paper folds
4. **A paper spaceship** — a sleek hybrid design combining swept-back fighter wings with elongated engine nacelles and a central fuselage. Should evoke "fast starfighter meets deep-space explorer" — **distinctly original**, not any recognizable franchise ship. All in paper-fold style.

## Style Guide
- **Medium:** Digital illustration, painterly but clean
- **Color palette:** Warm ambers, golds, deep browns, cream/parchment tones. The paper figures can be lighter — cream/white with warm shadows from the candlelight
- **Mood:** Intimate, inviting, magical, scholarly. The feeling of sitting down to read by candlelight and discovering that the stories are alive
- **Paper-fold aesthetic:** The origami/kirigami elements should look handcrafted — visible fold lines, geometric facets, paper texture. NOT flat cartoon cutouts. Think 3D paper sculpture rendered in illustration form
- **Composition:** Slightly angled top-down view (like you're standing over the desk looking down at the book). The book is the center, the candle anchors the right side, the figure anchors the left, the paper objects draw the eye upward

## Text
- "The Binding" — on the book cover in elegant, legible gold script
- "Enter the books you love." — can appear as a small tagline below the scene or integrated subtly

## Technical Requirements
- Final deliverable: High-resolution PNG (300 DPI minimum, 4000x2250px or larger for 16:9)
- Will be used for: Website hero image, social media, app splash screen, potential print materials
- Need both a wide (16:9) and square (1:1) crop if possible

## Reference Mood
- Warm candlelit Rembrandt-style lighting
- Paper craft art by artists like Jeff Nishinaka or Li Hongbo
- Fantasy RPG key art (D&D sourcebook covers, critical role artwork)
- Vintage book illustration (Arthur Rackham, Edmund Dulac)

## What NOT To Do
- No photorealistic 3D renders — this should feel illustrated
- No modern elements in the desk scene (no laptops, phones, etc.)
- No people besides the origami paper figure
- The spaceship should NOT look like any specific copyrighted ship
- Don't make the origami elements look flat — they should have dimension and fold depth
- Don't make "The Binding" text illegible or overly ornate

## Budget Context
This is for a startup game platform. Looking for quality concept art / illustration work. Happy to pay fair rates for good work. Expected range: $100-300 depending on artist experience and revision rounds.
